🛡️ Safety

Traffic Light System

The Traffic Light system is a simple and effective communication tool during a session. Green means everything is fine. Yellow indicates a need to slow down, adjust slightly, or check in — "I'm okay, but close to my limit." Red is an immediate and absolute stop, just like a safeword.

Safeword

A safeword is a pre-agreed word that stops the scene immediately. It should have no relation to the situation (e.g., "Avocado"). Saying it ends the activity immediately, without questions or punishment. It distinguishes between resistance that's part of the play ("No, please stop") and a real desire to stop.

Physical gesture

A physical gesture serves as a safeword when speech isn't possible (e.g., with a gag). Choose a clear gesture that's unmistakable, like three deliberate taps on the body or an agreed hand signal.

Hard limits

A hard limit is an absolute "no" that is non-negotiable. These are activities, words, or situations you will not participate in under any circumstances. Respecting hard limits is the foundation of trust and safety in a session.

Protection Methods

Physical & Mental Health

Sharing relevant medical information is crucial for safety. Inform about conditions like heart issues, asthma, past injuries, diabetes, or any other medical condition that could be affected by the activity. Mentally, it's important to mention known triggers, past traumas, or sensitivities to help your partner navigate the session with care and safety.

🦶 Impact (spanking, slapping, etc.)

Impact Play is any type of play involving controlled striking of the body, using hands or various implements. The goal is to create a physical sensation (from sharp to dull pain) and a psychological effect.

Paddle

A paddle is a flat, wide implement (usually wood or leather) that creates a shallow, thuddy pain. Especially effective on fleshy areas like the buttocks.

Caning

A cane is a thin, flexible stick (usually bamboo or rattan) that creates sharp, focused sensation and requires precision and safety awareness.

🛌 Aftercare

🧴 Immediate needs post-session

Aftercare is the process of physical and emotional support after a session. Its purpose is to help participants safely "land" from the intense experience, process emotions, return to themselves, and ensure everyone feels safe and valued. It is an essential part of responsible play.
